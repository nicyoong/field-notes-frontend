Brief

A lightweight cadence for insight reviews

A small weekly practice that keeps research signal high — without turning meetings into theatre.

Field Notes Oct 27, 2025 Topic: research ops

Premise

Most teams don’t have a research problem — they have an attention allocation problem. Insights exist, but they don’t land in the routines where decisions are made.

Cadence

  • 15 minutes weekly: one insight, one decision implication.
  • Rotate ownership: researchers, PMs, designers.
  • Capture in one place: a living “insights index”.

The smallest useful interview note

Keep notes oriented around claims, not transcripts: Observation → Interpretation → Implication.

If the note doesn’t change what you’d do next, it’s probably not a note — it’s a record.

— Internal research guide

Use this next

Try this for two weeks: require each “insight review” to end with one explicit decision question. If you can’t find one, it’s a signal the insight is not yet actionable — or the team’s strategy is unclear.
